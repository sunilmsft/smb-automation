Tim & Sunil — SMB Automation Project

Helping small businesses with simple automation & AI

Last updated: April 28, 2026

Project Overview

🎯 Mission

Find one real problem that small (mom-and-pop) businesses face and build a simple, affordable solution they will actually pay for.

📋 Constraints

  • Keep it simple — no over-engineering
  • Low cost to build and operate
  • Test with 2–5 real businesses quickly
  • Not trying to build a big startup right away
  • Starting in Seattle, expand based on traction

💡 Key Insight — Packaging Matters

SMB owners don't buy features — they buy solutions. We need to either:

Option A

Bundle 3–4 features under one theme (e.g., "Never Lose a Customer Again")

Recommended
Option B

Pick one very specific problem and absolutely nail it

Viable

📍 Seattle-First Strategy

  • In-person sales — walk in, shake hands, demo on a phone
  • Target neighborhoods: Ballard, Capitol Hill, Fremont, Columbia City, University District
  • Word of mouth travels fast in tight business communities
  • Expansion path: Seattle → Eastside (Bellevue, Kirkland, Redmond) → Tacoma → Portland

🏆 Current Recommendation

Idea #1 — "Never Miss a Lead" for home service businesses is the top pick based on ease of build, ease of sale, and clear ROI story.

Idea #2 — "Restaurant Customer Engine" is the strong alternative if you want a bigger long-term play in a market you're passionate about.

NEW — AI Phone Receptionist is the biggest untapped opportunity. Subsumes Idea #1 and has a massive "holy shit" factor.

Final decision pending discussion between Tim & Sunil.

⚠️ Trust Barrier Note

Ideas requiring bank account access (Invisible Bookkeeper, Cash Flow Crystal Ball) or operational control (Ghost Kitchen) need significant trust-building before SMBs will adopt. These are better as Phase 2/3 products once you have an established relationship with customers.

Out-of-the-Box Ideas

Bigger-swing ideas exploring untapped gaps in the SMB market. Ordered by opportunity size and feasibility.

🤖 #6 — AI Phone Receptionist Biggest Opportunity

Target: Every small business — start with dental offices, auto shops, law offices, med spas (where a missed call = $500+ lost)

Problem: A human receptionist costs $2,500–$4,000/mo. Small businesses can't afford one. So the owner answers while on a job, or nobody answers. AI voice quality in 2026 is now indistinguishable from human.

Solution: AI voice agent answers 24/7 — takes messages, answers FAQs ("What are your hours?", "Do you do free estimates?"), books appointments, routes urgent calls to owner's cell. English + Spanish.

Sales pitch: "We'll answer your phone like a real receptionist, 24/7, for less than you spend on coffee for the office."

Price
$199–$399/mo (vs $3K/mo human — 90% savings)
Market Size
33 million US small businesses; <5% can afford a receptionist
Build Complexity
★★★☆☆ Medium
Time to MVP
3–4 weeks
Competitors
Smith.ai, Ruby ($300–$700/mo, human). Bland.ai, Synthflow (dev tools, not plug-and-play)
Trust Barrier
Low — no bank access, no sensitive data

Why this is huge: The tech just crossed the threshold where this is possible for <$200/mo. Nobody has made it dead-simple for a non-technical business owner. This also subsumes Idea #1 — the receptionist answers the call instead of just texting back. It's strictly better.

Deep Dives

📞 Deep Dive: Idea #1 — Never Miss a Lead

Why It Wins

Simplest to buildTwilio number + webhook + SMS. No calendar, no CRM, no UI for v1
Easiest to prove ROI"You missed 47 calls last month. We saved 12." Dead simple
Fastest to sellEvery service business owner knows they miss calls. Zero education
Low costTwilio: ~$1/mo per number + $0.0079/SMS. 5 pilots for under $20/mo
ExpandableOnce trusted, bolt on reviews, follow-ups, scheduling → full platform

Tech Stack (v1)

  • Twilio — phone number + SMS
  • Simple webhook (Node.js or Python on a free tier)
  • Google Sheets — lead log (v1, replace with DB later)
  • No frontend needed for MVP

Pricing Model

$49–$99/mo per business. They're losing $500+/mo in missed leads — it's a no-brainer.

2-Week Validation Plan

Based on Idea #1 — Never Miss a Lead. Adjust if we pick a different idea.

🔨 Week 1 — Build + Find Businesses

Day 1–2
Build MVP
Twilio number → missed call detection → auto-SMS reply → log to Google Sheet. No app, no dashboard.
Day 3
Landing Page + Demo Setup
Simple 1-page site explaining the service. Get a burner Twilio number for live demos.
Day 4–5
Pound the Pavement
Walk into 10 local service businesses. Pitch: "I'll make sure you never lose a customer from a missed call again. Free for 2 weeks, then $49/mo." Goal: sign up 3–5.
Day 6–7
Onboard Pilot Customers
Set up call forwarding on no-answer to Twilio number. Customize each business's auto-reply message.

🔬 Week 2 — Run + Learn

Day 8–10
Monitor & Fix
Track daily: calls caught, texts sent, replies received. Fix edge cases — voicemail conflicts, after-hours timing, spam calls.
Day 11–12
Check-In With Businesses
"You missed 18 calls this week. 7 people texted back. Did any turn into jobs?" Collect stories and feedback.
Day 13–14
Decision Time
Do businesses see value? Will they pay $49/mo? If 2+ say yes → we have a business. If 0 → pivot to Idea #2 or #4.
