### Session 1 — April 28, 2026

**Participants:** Sunil + AI assistant

#### What We Discussed
1. Explored the idea of building automation/AI tools for small businesses
2. Identified 5 specific ideas with target customers and exact problems
3. Built a detailed comparison matrix across 10+ criteria
4. Debated single-feature vs. bundled-solution packaging
5. Decided on Seattle-first go-to-market strategy
6. Created a 2-week validation plan
7. Built a multi-tab HTML dashboard and deployed to GitHub Pages

#### Decisions Made
| Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Focus on SMB automation & AI | Both Tim & Sunil interested; large underserved market |
| Seattle-first strategy | Enables in-person sales; dense business districts; word-of-mouth |
| Bundle features under one theme | SMB owners buy solutions not features; higher perceived value |
| Top pick: "Never Miss a Lead" | Easiest to build (1–2 wks), easiest to sell, clearest ROI |
| Strong alt: Restaurant Engine | Bigger market, passionate space, but more complex to build |
| GitHub Pages for dashboard | Free hosting, easy to share with Tim, auto-updates on push |

#### Key Findings
- SMB owners don't buy features — they buy solutions to problems
- Competitors (Podium, Housecall Pro) charge $300–$500/mo — huge price gap to exploit
- Twilio costs are negligible (~$1/mo per number + $0.0079/SMS) — 5 pilots for <$20/mo
- Home service businesses miss 40%+ of calls while on jobs
- 20–30% no-show rate for appointment-based businesses
- Nobody owns the post-visit customer relationship for small restaurants affordably
- Seattle has 4,000+ home service businesses and 5,000+ independent restaurants
